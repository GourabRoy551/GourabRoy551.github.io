About me

AI engineer and Erasmus Mundus student working on trustworthy vision AI.

I am an Erasmus Mundus student in IPCVAI, Artificial Intelligence for Image Processing and Computer Vision, studying at UAM Madrid. My work sits at the intersection of computer vision, deep learning, and biomedical imaging, with a focus on models that are reliable, interpretable, and useful in real settings.

Current base

UAM Madrid, IPCVAI 2025-2027

Computer vision, medical AI, explainability, and multimodal learning.

Background

From teaching and research support to graduate AI research.

Before joining IPCVAI, I worked as an Adjunct Lecturer and Research Assistant in Computer Science and Engineering. I mentored thesis students, supported research projects, and contributed to work in medical image analysis, especially CT and MRI related AI applications.

I have also served as a reviewer for Informatics in Medicine Unlocked, which helped sharpen how I read research: not only looking for strong results, but also asking how carefully the problem is framed, how honestly the model is evaluated, and whether the method can be trusted outside a clean benchmark.

01 / Vision models

Vision Transformers and multimodal learning.

I am interested in how modern vision and vision-language models learn useful representations, especially when the task involves image-text alignment or limited domain data.

02 / Explainability

Interpretability for CLIP-style systems.

My current direction includes explainable AI for vision-language models, with a practical interest in explanations that help researchers inspect model behavior instead of simply decorating the output.

03 / Healthcare AI

Medical imaging segmentation and classification.

I care about AI that can support real healthcare research, especially CT and MRI workflows where reliability, data quality, and interpretability matter as much as raw accuracy.
